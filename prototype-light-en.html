   Description

The prototype is a waterproof robot designed to navigate bodies of water and perform water filtration as it moves. This robot will utilize tidal energy for its operation, leveraging the movement of the water to generate energy.


 Components


  • Incorporated Filters: â€¢ The robot is equipped with built-in filters that effectively remove suspended particles and contaminants from the water.

  • Chemical Compartments: The robot will include compartments to store and release chemicals such as chlorine, ozone, and potassium permanganate, which will facilitate the disinfection and purification of the water.

  • Propulsion System: It will enable the robot to move or swim in the water, utilising tidal energy.

  • Water Quality Sensors: Sensors that will measure parameters such as pH, conductivity, and the presence of contaminants, adjusting treatment as needed to ensure optimal water quality.

Functioning

  1. Movement and Filtration: e robot's movement and filtration system work in tandem to ensure the removal of particles and the improvement of water quality as the robot moves.
  2. Chemical Release: The compartments will release chemicals in controlled doses to disinfect and purify the water.
  3. Monitoring and Adjustment: Sensors will continuously assess water quality, allowing for real-time adjustments to optimize the purification process.



Expected Results

  • Safe Drinking Water: The combined use of filters and chemicals ensures that the water is safe for human consumption.

  • Improved Water Quality: Reduce contaminants and improve water quality parameters such as clarity and taste.

  • Sustainability: Use of renewable energy (tidal) to power the robot, contributing to the sustainability of the project.
